## Executive Summary

This content plan builds on the existing social content strategy (drafted February 2026) and outlines specific themes, campaigns, and content topics for Q2 2026 (April–June). The plan maintains the current lead generation focus while introducing new campaign themes designed to accelerate demo requests and deepen brand awareness in the SA private security market.

**Q2 Focus Areas:**
1. **Lead Generation Acceleration** — More direct CTAs and proof-point content
2. **Founder Voice Amplification** — Michael's thought leadership series
3. **Customer Proof Points** — Case studies and ROI stories
4. **Seasonal/Temporal Themes** — Q2-specific angles (mid-year planning, budget season)
5. **Community Impact** — Continuation of mission-driven content

**Target:** 3–5 demo requests attributed to social by end of Q2 (up from 1–2 target in Month 3 of original strategy)

## Campaign Concepts

### Campaign 1: "The Scanner Bill"

**Timing:** April (start of Q2)
**Format:** 3-post series
**Goal:** Drive cost-conversion leads

**Post 1:** "You're paying R360,000/year to rent devices your guards are trying to break."
**Post 2:** "What if I told you — the hardware you need is already on site?"
**Post 3:** "One client replaced R1,500/month scanners with R300/month WhatsApp. Here's what happened."

**CTA:** "See the maths" → demo booking page

---

### Campaign 2: "Proof Week"

**Timing:** May (mid Q2)
**Format:** 5-post series over one week
**Goal:** Demonstrate accountability value, drive demo requests

**Posts:**
- Monday: "What does 'proof of service' actually look like?"
- Tuesday: Video/screenshot of Export Flow PDF
- Wednesday: "How [Client] automated all their client reporting"
- Thursday: "GPS-verified patrol check-ins — live demo screenshot"
- Friday: "Book a demo and see your first automated report by Monday"

**CTA:** Demo booking each day

---

### Campaign 3: "Founder Stories" (Michael's Series)

**Timing:** Distributed across Q2 (2 posts/month)
**Format:** Text + personal photo or product screenshot
**Goal:** Humanise brand, establish thought leadership

**Topics:**
- "Why I left AI consulting to build safety infrastructure in South Africa"
- "The moment we realised WhatsApp was the answer"
- "What 500,000 licence discs taught us about data"
- "Building in Kayamandi — what we learned from township deployment"
- "The future of safety data: prediction, not reaction"
- "Why the scanner model is broken (and what comes next)"

**Tone:** Personal, authentic, slightly vulnerable. Not salesy.
