## Platform Strategy

### LinkedIn — The Anchor Platform

LinkedIn is where your audience lives: investors, founders, technical talent, security industry leaders, partners.

**What to post:**
- Founder journey stories (fundraising, acquisition, pivots, failures, wins)
- Technical deep-dives disguised as stories (architecture decisions, scaling challenges)
- Behind-the-scenes of building with AI (Cursor, AI-augmented workflows, 2-person team reality)
- Demo videos (product walkthroughs with your face + screen recording)
- Hot takes on the security industry, AI engineering, emerging market tech
- Milestone posts with context (not just "we hit X" — explain what it took)
- Carousel breakdowns of technical concepts or product features

**What not to post:**
- Generic congratulatory "excited to announce" posts
- Pure product marketing (that's the company page's job)
- Thought leadership that sounds like a McKinsey report
- AI slop — people can smell it instantly, especially on LinkedIn

**Format preferences:**
- Text posts: 1,200–1,500 characters. Hook in first line. Line breaks for readability.
- Video: 1–3 minutes. Talking head + screen share. Vertical (9:16) or square (1:1).
- Carousels: 5–10 slides. One idea per slide. Bold text. Clean design.
- Links in comments, never in the post body.

**Posting times:** Tuesday–Thursday, 7–8am SAST or 12pm SAST. Avoid weekends to start.

---

### Twitter/X — The Speed Platform

Twitter is where you build community with other founders, engage in real-time tech conversation, and share quick, punchy takes.

**What to post:**
- Quick wins and observations from building ("Shipped a new feature in 3 hours start to finish. AI-assisted code is genuinely changing my velocity.")
- Threads breaking down technical decisions or product architecture
- Hot takes on AI, safety tech, startup life, emerging markets
- Engagement with other founders and tech people (quote tweets, replies)
- Real-time commentary on relevant news (crime data releases, AI breakthroughs, SA tech)
- Memes and humour where appropriate (tech Twitter loves personality)

**What not to post:**
- Scheduled corporate content that feels dead on arrival
- Threads without a hook that grabs in the first tweet
- Pure self-promotion without value

**Format preferences:**
- Tweets: Under 200 characters for engagement; longer for threads
- Threads: 5–10 tweets. Hook in tweet 1. One idea per tweet. End with a CTA.
- Images: Screenshots of product, code, dashboards, maps
- Video: Short clips (30–60 seconds) — demo moments, behind-the-scenes

**Posting times:** More flexible. Morning, lunch, evening. Twitter rewards frequency and real-time presence.

---

### TikTok — The Amplifier

TikTok is where you reach entirely new audiences who would never find you on LinkedIn. The algorithm rewards content quality over follower count — a video from an account with 50 followers can hit 500,000 views if the content is good.

You're comfortable on camera. That's the unlock. Most B2B founders can't do this.

**What to post:**
- **Product demos** (30–60 sec): "Watch me scan a licence disc using just WhatsApp" — show the magic moment
- **Day in the life** (30–90 sec): What being a CTO at a safety startup actually looks like
- **Technical explainers** (30–60 sec): "How we built X" — bite-sized architecture explanations
- **Behind-the-scenes** (15–60 sec): Office, team, deployments, township visits, demo setups
- **Storytelling** (30–90 sec): The Namola acquisition story, the first deployment story, the "guards breaking devices" story
- **Reactions/commentary**: React to safety/crime/tech content with your perspective
- **"People don't know this about..." format**: "People don't know this about private security in South Africa..."

**What not to post:**
- Overly produced corporate content — TikTok rewards authenticity and rawness
- Content that requires context to understand (assume the viewer knows nothing)
- Hard selling

**Format:**
- Vertical (9:16) only
- Hook in first 1–2 seconds (text on screen + spoken)
- Captions/subtitles always on
- Trending sounds where they fit naturally
- Keep to 30–60 seconds to start; extend as you learn what holds attention

**Posting times:** 7–9am, 12–3pm, 7–11pm SAST. Consistency matters more than timing.

## Video Content Playbook

Since you're comfortable with a camera, video is your biggest unfair advantage. Here's a practical playbook.

### Equipment (Keep It Simple)
- Your phone (iPhone in good lighting is more than enough)
- A cheap clip-on mic (Boya, Rode, or similar — ~R500) for cleaner audio
- Natural light or a ring light if filming indoors
- A phone tripod/holder for stable shots

### Video Types to Film in Batches

**1. Product Demo (30–60 sec)**
- Screen record the WhatsApp interaction + film yourself narrating
- Or: selfie-view talking + picture-in-picture of the screen
- Always open with: "Let me show you..." or "Watch what happens when..."

**2. Talking Head / Opinion (15–45 sec)**
- Look into the camera. One idea. Deliver it with conviction.
- Hook (text on screen): "Security companies are paying R360,000/year for devices guards destroy."
- Deliver the take. End with a question or a mic-drop line.

**3. Behind-the-Scenes (15–60 sec)**
- Film your desk, your screens, a deployment, a drive through a neighbourhood, the team
- Narrate over it: "This is what building a safety startup in South Africa actually looks like."
- Raw and unpolished > slick and produced

**4. Day-in-the-Life (60–90 sec)**
- Morning: quick clip. Meeting: quick clip. Coding: quick clip. Deployment: quick clip.
- Stitch them together with a voiceover or on-screen text.
- This format performs extremely well on TikTok.

**5. Storytime (30–90 sec)**
- Sit down, look into the camera, tell a story.
- "Last month we acquired Namola. Here's what that was actually like."
- "A guard put a R15,000 scanner in a sock and bashed it against the wall. That's when I knew we were building the right thing."

### Video Editing (Minimal)
- CapCut (free, excellent for TikTok/Reels/Shorts format)
- Auto-captions via CapCut or TikTok's native tool — always add captions
- Simple cuts to remove dead air — no fancy transitions needed
- Text on screen for hooks and key points

## Voice & Personality Guide

### Who you are on social:
A technical co-founder building safety data infrastructure in South Africa. You code, you demo, you deploy. You acquired a company. You're raising money. You've built a multi-product platform with a tiny team and AI. You have opinions and you've earned the right to share them.

### How to sound:

**Do:**
- First person. "I built this." "We shipped this." "I was wrong about X."
- Specific. Numbers, timelines, real details. "500,000 scans" not "lots of scans."
- Direct. Short sentences. One idea per line. Let points breathe.
- Honest. Share what's hard, not just what's working.
- South African. Don't code-switch for an American audience. Your context is your advantage.
- Conversational. Write like you'd explain it over a braai, not in a boardroom.

**Don't:**
- Use corporate speak ("synergies", "leveraging", "at the intersection of")
- Start every post with "I'm excited to announce..."
- Write long paragraphs without line breaks
- Sound like you're performing humility ("Humbled to announce...")
- Over-explain who you are — build context post by post
- Be preachy or lecture-y — share discoveries, don't give TED talks

### Your unique angles (lean into these):
1. **You actually built the thing.** Most founders on LinkedIn talk about building. You can show it.
2. **You're in an emerging market.** That's not a disadvantage — it's a perspective most people don't have.
3. **You're AI-native in practice.** Not "exploring AI" — you've built an entire platform with it.
4. **You have real deployment stories.** Townships, boom gates, control rooms. Not theoretical.
5. **You acquired a company.** M&A stories at seed stage are rare and fascinating.

## Content Repurposing Flow

One experience → multiple pieces of content:

```
Film a product demo (2 min raw)
    ├── TikTok: 30-sec punchy edit with hook
    ├── LinkedIn: Same video or shorter cut + 200-word context post
    ├── Twitter: Screen-grab + thread explaining the feature
    └── Company page: Product-focused version for lead gen
```

```
Make a technical decision
    ├── LinkedIn: "Here's why we chose X over Y" (story format)
    ├── Twitter: Quick thread with the decision + reasoning
    ├── TikTok: 30-sec talking head "We had to choose between X and Y. Here's what we picked and why."
    └── Blog (future): Longer-form write-up
```

```
Hit a milestone
    ├── LinkedIn: Milestone + the story behind it + what you learned
    ├── Twitter: Quick celebratory tweet + a thread on the journey
    ├── TikTok: Behind-the-scenes footage from the moment
    └── Company page: Customer-facing proof point
```

## What NOT to Post (Personal Brand)

- **"We're hiring!" without context** — If you post about hiring, tell the story of why
- **Vague inspiration** — "The grind is real. Keep pushing." — means nothing without specifics
- **Engagement bait** — "Agree?" with nothing behind it
- **Overly polished corporate content** — This isn't the company page
- **Complaining without insight** — Frustration is fine; make it useful
- **Anything that sounds AI-generated** — Ironic given your pillar, but people can tell. Write in your voice or don't post it.
- **Competitor bashing by name** — Take the high road; let the product speak
- **Sensitive deployment details** — No client names without permission, no guard names, no specific locations of active security operations
